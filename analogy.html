Analogy

General types

Part to Whole - a pair of words consists of a part and a whole. For example, spoke : wheel. A spoke is part of a wheel.

Type and Category - pairs of words in which one word is a specific type in a general category.

Degree of Intensity - todiscern nuance of meaning among pairs of words.For example, shower : monsoon. A shower is light rainfall and a monsoon is heavy rainfall.

Function - pair words that are related through function. For example, hammer : build. A hammer is used to build.

Manner - describes the manner, way, or style by which an action is accomplished. For example, shamble : walk. Shamble means to walk in an awkward manner.

Symbol or representation - pair words in which one word is the symbol of the other. For example,dove : peace. A dove is a symbol of peace.

Action and significanceone word describes an action and the other word indicates the significance of the action. For example, cry : sorrow. To cry signifies sorrow.
